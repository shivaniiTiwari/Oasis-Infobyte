Biography

Virat Kohli ( born 5 November 1988) is an Indian international cricketer and the former captain of the Indian national cricket team who plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and for the Delhi in Indian domestic cricket. Widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of all time,[4] Kohli holds the records for scoring most runs in T20 internationals and in the IPL. In 2020, the International Cricket Council named him the male cricketer of the decade. Kohli has also contributed to a number of India's successes, including winning the 2011 World Cup and the 2013 Champions trophy.

Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi to a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer and his mother, Saroj Kohli, served as a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikas, and an older sister, Bhawna.[5] Kohli's formative years were spent in the Uttam Nagar and commenced his early education at Vishal Bharti Public School.[6] According to his family, Kohli exhibited an early affinity for cricket as a mere three-year-old. He would pick up a cricket bat, display natural skill, and request his father to bowl to him..

  • 2011/12

    That Hobart chase against Sri Lanka in Australia tri-series

    With 12,169 runs at an average of 59 including 43 centuries and 62 half-centuries, Kohli has established himself as one of the greatest ever players and he started his career with ODIs. The watershed moment in his career came in the 2011/12 tri-series match against Sri Lanka. It was a must-win game and India needed to win the match with a bonus point to qualify for the final, which Australia had already made to. Tillakaratne Dilshan and Kumar Sangakkara's counter-attack meant India had to chase down 320 runs in 40 overs. Sehwag, Sachin, Gautam Gambhir all gave starts and played crucial innings but it was Virat Kohli's night. Every Sri Lankan bowler went the distance, Kohli played one of the most calculative-explosive knocks of ODI history. It was the beginning of the chase master, which Virat Kohli has become over the years. Kohli played an unbeaten knock of 133 runs off just 86 balls, hitting 16 fours and a couple of sixes as India chased the total down in just 36.4 overs.

  • 2018

    Johannesburg Test win against South Africa

    This isn't about Kohli the batsman, but the captain. Test wins don't come easy, especially the overseas ones, that too in SENA countries and above all in South Africa. South Africa had won the first two games of the series and had taken a 2-0 unassailable lead in the three-match series. This was the first series where India unleashed the trio of Mohammed Shami, Jasprit Bumrah and Ishant Sharma on the opponents. They were able to take 40 wickets in the first two games and needed to take 20 more and with some help of their batsmen, they could inspire India to a win at Johannesburg, where the ball was flying, seaming, swinging and doing all sorts of things. Kohli and Pujara smashed half-centuries but India were bowled out for 187 runs batting first. India bowlers replied in style Bumrah taking a fifer and Bhuvneshwar Kumar three wickets, bowling the hosts for 194. A seven-run lead wasn't much but India needed a strong batting performance. Another good score of 41 by Kohli and contributions from Rahane, Shami and Bhuvneshwar meant, India scored 247 runs setting up a challenging 241-run target for South Africa on a deteriorating surface, This time Shami took a fifer as India's four-pronged pace battery dismissed South Africa for just 177 as the visitors won a Test in South Africa..
